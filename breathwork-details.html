Breathwork

Conscious Breathwork is an ancient practice that uses specific breathing techniques to facilitate profound healing, emotional release, and spiritual awakening. Your breath is a bridge between your conscious and unconscious mind, your body and spirit.

Through powerful breathing patterns, we can bypass the analytical mind and connect directly with your inner wisdom, allowing suppressed emotions and limiting beliefs to surface and be released.

Each breathwork session creates a safe container for exploration and healing, guided by the intelligence of your breath and the wisdom of your body.

How Breathwork Works

1. Conscious Breathing

Using specific breathing patterns to access altered states of consciousness and bypass the analytical mind.

2. Emotional Release

Allowing suppressed emotions and limiting beliefs to surface and be released from the body and mind.

3. Integration

Processing and integrating the insights and healing that occurred, creating lasting transformation.

What to Expect

Preparation for Your Session

Come with an open mind and comfortable clothing. We'll create a safe, supportive environment for your breathing journey.

Preparations:
  • Download the Zoom app. You'll receive a Zoom link approximately 30 minutes before we start.
  • Prepare a place where you can lie comfortably and undisturbed.
  • I recommend external speakers or headphones for good sound. Please test beforehand so everything works and is charged.
  • Wear loose, comfortable clothing
  • Avoid eating a heavy meal 2 hours before
  • Bring water
  • Set an intention for your session

During Your Session

We'll begin with guided breathing techniques and create a safe container for your experience. Allow yourself to breathe, feel, and release whatever needs to move through you.

After Your Session

Take time to integrate your experience. Stay hydrated, rest, and be gentle with yourself as you process any insights or emotions that arose.

Important Considerations

There are certain conditions where practicing breathwork may not be appropriate.

  • Pregnancy
  • Cardiovascular diseases
  • Epilepsy
  • Asthma and other respiratory conditions
  • Mental health conditions
